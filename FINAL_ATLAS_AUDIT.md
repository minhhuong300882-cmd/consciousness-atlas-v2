## 1. COVERAGE EVALUATION

**Score: 78/100**

### Strengths
- **10 Major Consciousness Theories**: Integrated Information Theory, Global Workspace Theory, Higher-Order Thought, Predictive Processing, Active Inference, Self Model Theory, Affective Consciousness, Neurophenomenology, Constructed Emotion, Embodied Cognition
- **12 Core Concepts**: Consciousness, Self, Emotion, Perception, Attention, Memory, Prediction, Body, Reality, Agency, Free Will, Identity
- **6 Major Debates**: Hard Problem, Binding Problem, Free Will vs Determinism, Animal Consciousness, AI Consciousness, Self Reality
- **28 Curated Books**: Organized by difficulty level and category
- **6 Key Researchers**: Damasio, Seth, Metzinger, Tononi, Dehaene, Chalmers
- **4 Learning Paths**: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Researcher (60+ modules)

### Weaknesses
- **Limited Author Coverage**: Only 6 of 20+ major consciousness researchers represented
- **Shallow Theory Depth**: Each theory has 14 dimensions but lacks experimental evidence links
- **Missing Phenomena**: Sleep, anesthesia, psychedelics, meditation, altered states not covered
- **No Emerging Theories**: Recent work in neurophenomenology variants, quantum consciousness, etc. absent
- **Limited Neuroscience Detail**: Brain regions, neural mechanisms underrepresented

### Missing Components
- Evidence Atlas (empirical studies by phenomenon)
- Researcher interviews or video content
- Interactive simulations of theories
- Historical evolution of theories
- Consciousness in non-human systems (detailed)

## 3. INTERCONNECTIVITY EVALUATION

**Score: 85/100**

### Strengths
- **Bidirectional Cross-Links**: Theory ↔ Concept, Theory ↔ Author, Theory ↔ Debate, Theory ↔ Book, Concept ↔ Debate, Concept ↔ Book
- **Unified Cross-Link Component**: Reusable display system across all sections
- **Network Visualization**: Interactive concept network showing 25+ relationships
- **Multiple Navigation Paths**: Users can enter from any section and navigate to related content
- **Consistent Link Styling**: Color-coded by type (theory, concept, author, debate, book)

### Weaknesses
- **One-Way Links in Some Cases**: Author → Theory links exist but Theory → Author links incomplete
- **Missing Link Types**: No Theory ↔ Learning Path links, no Concept ↔ Learning Path links
- **Limited Link Context**: Links show title but not why they're related
- **No Link Strength Weighting**: All relationships treated equally (some more important than others)
- **No Backlink Tracking**: Can't see "what links to this theory"

### Missing Components
- Bidirectional Author ↔ Theory links
- Learning Path ↔ Concept/Theory/Debate connections
- Link strength/importance indicators
- Relationship type labels (e.g., "supports", "contradicts", "extends")
- Backlink navigation
