## 1. FACTUAL ACCURACY AUDIT (78/100)

### Strengths
- Theory descriptions are generally accurate
- Major consciousness theories correctly represented
- Historical timeline is accurate (1600s-2020s progression)
- Key researchers properly credited

### Weaknesses

#### 1.1 Libet Readiness Potential - MEDIUM SEVERITY
**Issue:** Description presents Libet's original interpretation as more definitive than current consensus.  
**Current:** "Suggests consciousness may not initiate action but rather becomes aware of actions already initiated"  
**Problem:** Schurger's 2012 reanalysis significantly challenged this interpretation, showing readiness potential may be statistical artifact  
**Fix:** Update to: "Libet's original interpretation has been challenged by Schurger (2012), who argues readiness potential may be statistical artifact rather than evidence of unconscious intention. Current consensus: interpretation remains controversial."  
**Severity:** Medium  
**Effort:** 30 minutes

#### 1.2 Global Workspace Theory - LOW SEVERITY
**Issue:** Description emphasizes Baars' original formulation but doesn't mention Dehaene's computational extensions  
**Current:** Mentions Baars and Dehaene but doesn't distinguish their contributions  
**Problem:** Dehaene's "Global Neuronal Workspace" is distinct mathematical framework  
**Fix:** Add separate section: "Dehaene's Computational Extension: Adds mathematical formalism and predicts specific neural signatures (late positive component in EEG)"  
**Severity:** Low  
**Effort:** 1 hour

#### 1.3 Integrated Information Theory (IIT) - MEDIUM SEVERITY
**Issue:** Doesn't mention Tononi's recent revisions (IIT 3.0, 4.0)  
**Current:** References IIT but doesn't specify version or evolution  
**Problem:** IIT has evolved significantly; current version addresses earlier criticisms  
**Fix:** Add: "IIT Evolution: Version 1.0 (2004) → 2.0 (2006) → 3.0 (2012) → 4.0 (2015). Current version addresses criticisms about phi calculation and consciousness definition."  
**Severity:** Medium  
**Effort:** 2 hours

#### 1.4 Predictive Processing - LOW SEVERITY
**Issue:** Conflates "Predictive Processing" with "Predictive Coding" and "Free Energy Principle"  
**Current:** Uses terms interchangeably  
**Problem:** These are related but distinct frameworks with different implications  
**Fix:** Clarify: "Predictive Processing (umbrella term) → Predictive Coding (mechanism) → Free Energy Principle (mathematical framework by Friston) → Active Inference (behavioral implementation)"  
**Severity:** Low  
**Effort:** 1.5 hours

#### 1.5 Neurophenomenology - LOW SEVERITY
**Issue:** Understates Varela's emphasis on first-person methodology  
**Current:** Brief mention without emphasizing methodological innovation  
**Problem:** Varela's key contribution was integrating first-person reports with neuroscience  
**Fix:** Expand: "Neurophenomenology's core innovation: systematic integration of first-person phenomenological reports with third-person neuroscientific data to constrain consciousness theories"  
**Severity:** Low  
**Effort:** 1 hour

## 2. CITATION QUALITY AUDIT (71/100)

### Strengths
- Primary citations for major theories included
- Key researchers properly attributed
- Landmark experiments cited

### Weaknesses

#### 2.1 Missing Primary Citations - HIGH SEVERITY
**Issue:** Many theory descriptions lack DOI/journal references  
**Current:** ~60% of theory descriptions have citations, 40% lack them  
**Problem:** Reduces verifiability and research usefulness  
**Missing Citations:**
- Self Model Theory (Metzinger) - needs "Being No One" (2003) reference
- Affective Consciousness (Solms) - needs primary papers
- Embodied Cognition (Damasio) - needs "Descartes' Error" (1994) reference

```

- Metzinger, T. (2003). Being No One: The Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity. MIT Press. ISBN: 0262134721
- Metzinger, T. (2009). The Ego Tunnel: The Science of the Mind and the Myth of the Self. Basic Books.
```

**Severity:** High  
**Effort:** 3-4 hours

#### 2.2 Secondary Literature Missing - MEDIUM SEVERITY
**Issue:** No review articles or meta-analyses cited  
**Current:** Only primary research papers cited  
**Problem:** Doesn't provide entry points for students to understand broader context  
**Fix:** Add "Review Articles" section for each theory with 2-3 key reviews:
```
Global Workspace Theory - Key Reviews:
- Dehaene, S., & Changeux, J. P. (2011). Experimental and theoretical approaches to conscious processing. Neuroscience, 12(3), 158-164.
- Baars, B. J., & Franklin, S. (2007). An architectural model of conscious and unconscious brain functions. Journal of Consciousness Studies, 14(4), 31-48.
```

**Severity:** Medium  
**Effort:** 4-5 hours

**Current:** ~70% of citations pre-2015, ~30% 2015-2020, ~5% 2020+  
**Problem:** Misses recent developments and current consensus shifts  
**Missing Recent Work:**
- Anil Seth's "Being You" (2021) - major popular science synthesis
- Friston's recent active inference papers (2021-2025)
- Solms' recent work on affect and consciousness (2021-2024)
- Dehaene's recent neuroimaging studies (2020-2025)

**Fix:** Add "Recent Developments (2020-2026)" section to each theory with 2-3 recent papers  
**Severity:** Medium  
**Effort:** 3-4 hours

#### 2.4 Controversial Claims Lack Citations - MEDIUM SEVERITY
**Issue:** Some claims about theory criticisms lack supporting citations  
**Current:** Criticisms listed without references  
**Problem:** Reduces credibility of critique  
**Examples:**
- "Readiness potential may be statistical artifact" - cite Schurger 2012
- "IIT's phi calculation is problematic" - cite specific critics

**Severity:** Medium
